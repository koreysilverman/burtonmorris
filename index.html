The Artist

FROM PITTSBURGH
TO THE WORLD STAGE

Internationally acclaimed Pop Artist Burton Morris is renowned for his bold, high-energy interpretations of pop culture iconography. His signature style—characterized by radiant black outlines, amplified colors, and striking compositions—transforms everyday objects into vibrant icons of contemporary life.

Born in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania in 1964, he earned his BFA at Carnegie Mellon University in 1986. He established Burton Morris Studios in 1990, choosing one subject per composition to create "an instant happening" for the viewer—blurring the lines between high and low art.

In 1992, Absolut Vodka selected his artwork alongside Warhol and Haring. In 1994, his paintings debuted on "Friends," displayed in Central Perk for over ten seasons. His "destruct" style—vibrating canvases caught mid-motion—mirrors the rapid rhythm of contemporary life.

Beyond art, Morris has raised millions for charitable causes worldwide.

Featured Exhibition

THE ART OF FRIENDS

"The Art of Friends", an exhibition by Burton Morris in association with Warner Bros Inc., pays tribute to the artist's creative contributions to the television show that became a pop culture phenomenon. The show debuted at Taglialatella Galleries in Chelsea, New York City.

Since Friends premiered in September 1994, more than a dozen of Morris' original pop art paintings were featured on set in the colorful Central Perk coffee shop throughout the sitcom's ten seasons. The millions of fans directly associated the bold, bright aesthetic of Morris' artwork with the characters and storylines of the beloved series.

Morris remembers when his sister called from California to say she saw David Schwimmer (Ross) wearing a tee shirt featuring his artwork. "He grabbed the shirt from a staff member right before filming the scene. Call it luck or good timing, but the next day I made a call to Warner Brothers Studios and was able to reach Kevin Bright, one of the creators of the show."
